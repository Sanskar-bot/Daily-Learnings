#### Security Frameworks Studied
- **NIST Cybersecurity Framework** — the gold standard for organizational security posture, organized into 5 core functions:
  - **Identify** — asset management, risk assessment, governance
  - **Protect** — access control, awareness training, data security
  - **Detect** — anomaly detection, continuous monitoring, event analysis
  - **Respond** — incident response planning, communications, mitigation
  - **Recover** — recovery planning, improvements, communications
- **ISO/IEC 27001** — international standard for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS)
  - Focused on risk management and continuous improvement
  - Requires organizations to establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve an ISMS
  - Certification demonstrates compliance to clients and regulators
- **CERT-In (Indian Computer Emergency Response Team)** — India's nodal agency for cybersecurity incident response
  - Operates under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY)
  - Mandates incident reporting within 6 hours of detection (as of April 2022 directive)
  - Issues advisories, vulnerability notes, and threat alerts for Indian organizations

#### Types of Cybersecurity
- **Network Security** — firewalls, IDS/IPS, VPNs — protecting the network perimeter and internal traffic
  - Firewalls filter traffic based on rules; IDS detects intrusions; IPS actively blocks them
- **Endpoint Security** — antivirus, EDR (Endpoint Detection & Response), device management — securing individual devices
  - Modern EDR goes beyond signature-based detection — uses behavioral analysis to detect zero-day threats
- **Cloud Security** — IAM policies, encryption at rest/in transit, shared responsibility model
  - Shared responsibility: cloud provider secures the infrastructure; the customer secures their data and configurations
- **Application Security** — secure coding practices, OWASP Top 10, WAFs (Web Application Firewalls)
  - OWASP Top 10: injection, broken authentication, XSS, insecure deserialization, etc.
- **Data Security** — encryption, DLP (Data Loss Prevention), access controls — protecting data wherever it lives
  - DLP systems monitor and prevent unauthorized data transfers (email, USB, cloud uploads)
- **Identity & Access Management (IAM)** — MFA, RBAC, SSO — controlling who accesses what
  - Principle of least privilege: every user gets the minimum access needed to perform their job
