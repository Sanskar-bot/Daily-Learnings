#### Definition & Importance of Cybersecurity
- **Cybersecurity** — the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information
- Importance spans across industries: healthcare, finance, government, defense — anywhere digital data exists
- The growing attack surface due to IoT, cloud adoption, and remote work makes cybersecurity critical infrastructure
- India's digital transformation (UPI, Aadhaar, DigiLocker) has made cybersecurity a national priority — not just an IT concern
- Global cybercrime damages projected to reach **$10.5 trillion annually by 2025** (Cybersecurity Ventures) — emphasizing the scale of the threat

#### CIA Triad — The Foundation of Information Security
- **Confidentiality** — ensuring data is accessible only to authorized individuals (encryption, access controls)
  - Real-world example: patient medical records should only be accessible to authorized doctors and the patient themselves
  - Controls: AES encryption, role-based access control (RBAC), data classification policies
- **Integrity** — ensuring data is accurate and unaltered (hashing, digital signatures, checksums)
  - Real-world example: a lab report must not be tampered with between the lab system and the doctor's screen
  - Controls: SHA-256 hashing, digital signatures, file integrity monitoring (FIM)
- **Availability** — ensuring systems and data are accessible when needed (redundancy, backups, DDoS protection)
  - Real-world example: hospital systems must be online 24/7 — downtime directly impacts patient care
  - Controls: redundant servers, load balancers, disaster recovery plans, UPS systems
- Every cybersecurity incident can be analyzed through the lens of which CIA pillar was compromised
- The triad is not just theory — it's the lens through which security audits, incident response, and compliance frameworks operate

#### Why Cybersecurity Matters in Healthcare
- Healthcare is the **most targeted industry** for ransomware attacks globally
- Medical records are worth **10–40x more** than credit card data on the dark web (because they contain PII + insurance + medical history — all permanent, unlike a replaceable card number)
- Cyberattacks on hospitals can directly endanger lives — disrupted systems mean delayed surgeries, lost lab results, and inaccessible patient histories
- India's healthcare sector has seen a **sharp increase** in attacks post-COVID due to rapid digitization without proportional security investment
