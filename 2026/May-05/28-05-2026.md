### AIIMS Delhi Ransomware Attack — Case Study Research (Started)

> **Context:** After completing the Cybersecurity PPT, started a personal case study
> on one of India's most significant cyberattacks — the AIIMS Delhi Ransomware Attack
> of November 2022. This is publicly documented and widely covered by media and
> security researchers.

#### 1. Attack Overview
- **Date:** November 23, 2022
- **Target:** All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS), New Delhi — India's premier medical institution
- **Threat Actor:** ChamelGang — a Chinese Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) group
- **Impact:** Entire Hospital Information System (HIS) paralyzed for 15+ days

**Key Impact Metrics:**
- **Downtime:** 15+ days of complete digital system outage
- **Servers Compromised:** ~100 servers encrypted across the network
- **Patient Records Affected:** ~40 million patient records exfiltrated before encryption
- **Ransom Demanded:** ₹200 crore in cryptocurrency
- **Operations Affected:** OPD, ICU, labs, radiology, billing, and appointment systems
- **Data on Dark Web:** VVIP and political figures' medical records listed for sale

#### 2. How the Attack Began — Attack Chain Analysis
- A hospital staff member received a **spear-phishing email** crafted to impersonate an internal department or trusted vendor
- The email contained a malicious attachment that silently installed a backdoor (likely via **BeaconLoader**)
- Attackers spent days/weeks in **reconnaissance** — mapping the network, identifying high-value servers, harvesting credentials using **Mimikatz**
- Once domain administrator access was achieved, they deployed **CatB ransomware** simultaneously across 100+ servers
- **Backups were deliberately targeted and corrupted** before the ransomware activated — eliminating any clean recovery path

#### 3. CIA Triad Analysis of the Breach

| CIA Pillar | Breach Details | Root Cause |
|---|---|---|
| **Confidentiality** | 40 million patient records stolen; VVIP data appeared on dark web | Data stored in plaintext — no encryption at rest, no DLP system |
| **Integrity** | Backups corrupted alongside primary servers; no clean copy survived | No File Integrity Monitoring (FIM); backups on same network |
| **Availability** | 100+ servers encrypted; all digital services offline for 15+ days | No hot standby systems, no network segmentation, no DR plan |

**Why All Three Pillars Failed Simultaneously:**
- Confidentiality failed because data was stored without encryption and no DLP existed to detect mass exfiltration
- Integrity failed because backups lived on the same network as production — when production was compromised, so were backups
- Availability failed because there was no segmentation, no tested disaster recovery plan, and no failover infrastructure
