#### Re-Presentation Preparation — Mitigation Strategies for AIIMS Report

Prepared additional content for the re-presentation based on mentor feedback:

**Immediate Priority Actions:**
- Deploy MFA immediately on all remote access, privileged accounts, and cloud services
- Isolate and air-gap backup systems from the primary production network within 30 days
- Enable Windows Credential Guard to prevent Mimikatz-style memory scraping attacks
- Conduct a privileged account audit — enumerate all domain admin accounts and remove unnecessary privileges

**Preventive Controls:**
- Network segmentation — isolate clinical systems, admin networks, and backup infrastructure
- Encrypt all patient records and sensitive databases with AES-256 at rest
- Implement a formal patch management lifecycle — critical patches within 72 hours

**Detection Mechanisms:**
- Deploy File Integrity Monitoring (FIM) across all critical servers
- Email security gateway with advanced phishing detection
- Quarterly phishing simulations and mandatory security awareness training

**Incident Response Recommendations:**
- Establish a tested disaster recovery plan with defined RTO/RPO targets
- Maintain offline/immutable backups following the 3-2-1 strategy (3 copies, 2 different media, 1 offsite)
- Deploy hot standby systems for critical infrastructure

#### Key Conclusions from the AIIMS Case Study

Three lessons that stand above all others:
1. **Healthcare data is geopolitical data** — the exposure of VVIP medical records demonstrates hospitals are now targets of nation-state espionage
2. **The chain is only as strong as its weakest link** — a single phishing email compromised the nation's premier medical institution
3. **Recovery without preparation is impossible** — the 15+ day outage resulted from backups on the same network and no operational continuity plan
