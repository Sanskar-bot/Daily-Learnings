#### SingHealth Attack — Singapore (2018)

In July 2018, Singapore's largest public healthcare group SingHealth suffered a state-sponsored cyberattack that stole the personal and outpatient prescription records of 1.5 million patients — a quarter of the country's entire population — with Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong's medical records specifically and repeatedly targeted, in what investigators concluded was a deliberate act of geopolitical espionage linked to China, not financial crime.

- **Scale:** 1.5 million patient records stolen — Singapore's largest cyberattack in history
- **PM targeted:** Lee Hsien Loong's prescriptions and personal data were the specific, repeated focus of the breach
- **Method:** Attackers compromised a single front-end workstation, then escalated to full database access via stolen credentials
- **Motive:** China-linked APT — data used as geopolitical leverage in Sino-Singapore trade negotiations, not sold on dark web
- **Human failure:** A middle manager failed to escalate the breach out of fear — CISA could have stopped it in time
- **Classified as an Advanced Persistent Threat** — 8-day operation, June 27 to July 4, 2018

#### Change Healthcare Attack — United States (2024)

In February 2024, the BlackCat/ALPHV ransomware group attacked Change Healthcare — a subsidiary of UnitedHealth Group processing 15 billion medical transactions annually — encrypting systems that handle pharmacy billing and insurance claims across the entire United States, affecting an estimated 192.7 million individuals and making it the largest healthcare data breach in recorded history.

- **Scale:** 192.7 million individuals affected — the largest healthcare breach ever recorded, surpassing Anthem's 78.8M (2015)
- **Systemic impact:** Pharmacies, hospitals, and insurers nationwide could not process claims or prescriptions for weeks
- **Entry point:** A single set of stolen credentials with no MFA enabled — the attacker walked straight in
- **Ransom paid:** UnitedHealth Group paid $22 million in Bitcoin — attackers then extorted them a second time
- **Ascension Health (May 2024):** Black Basta ransomware simultaneously hit 142 hospitals, crippling clinical operations
- **Regulatory fallout:** US Senate grilled UnitedHealth CEO; Biden administration proposed $800M in hospital cybersecurity funding
